About Morning Pages

Morning Pages is a concept developed by Julia Cameron in her book, "The Artist's Way". It is a simple and powerful technique for clearing the mind, reducing stress, and boosting creativity.

What are Morning Pages?

Morning Pages are three pages of longhand, stream-of-consciousness writing, done first thing in the morning. The idea is to write without any thought of structure, grammar, or spelling. You simply write down whatever comes to mind, without editing or censoring yourself in any way.

The purpose of Morning Pages is to clear your mind of any distractions, worries, or anxieties that may be weighing on you. By getting these thoughts down on paper, you free up mental space and make room for new ideas and insights to emerge.

How do you do Morning Pages?

To do Morning Pages, all you need is a pen and paper or in this case just your computer . Sit down first thing in the morning, before you do anything else, and start writing. Write three pages, or for about 30 minutes, whichever comes first. Don't worry about what you're writing or whether it makes sense - just keep your hand moving and let the words flow.

You can do Morning Pages every day, or whenever you feel the need to clear your mind. Some people find that doing Morning Pages first thing in the morning sets the tone for the rest of the day, while others prefer to do them later in the day or before bed.

What are the benefits of Morning Pages?

The benefits of Morning Pages are numerous. They can help you:

  • Clear your mind of distractions and worries
  • Reduce stress and anxiety
  • Boost creativity and generate new ideas
  • Improve focus and productivity
  • Gain clarity and insight into your thoughts and feelings

Whether you're an artist, writer, or just someone looking to reduce stress and increase clarity in your life, Morning Pages can be a powerful tool for improving your mental health and well-being.
